I am a Research Scientist (*Chargé de Recherche*, CRCN) at [Inria Saclay](https://www.inria.fr/en/centre-inria-saclay-ile-de-france), a member of the [AnankΞ](https://team.inria.fr/ananke/) team hosted at [CMAP](https://portail.polytechnique.edu/cmap/en), École polytechnique.

My research lies at the intersection of **mathematical analysis**, **numerical analysis**, and **scientific computing** for partial differential equations (PDEs). I focus primarily on wave propagation problems and develop both analytical tools (asymptotic analysis, functional analysis) and robust numerical methods (spectral element methods, implicit-explicit schemes) for their study. A significant part of my activity concerns **inverse problems** motivated by medical imaging — in particular elastography and hemodynamic monitoring — and non-destructive testing.

## Research interests

- **Multi-scale analysis of wave propagation**: asymptotic analysis of PDEs in thin or elongated domains, homogenization, double-scale problems (piezoelectricity, electromagnetism in cables, gravito-acoustics, incompressible elastodynamics).
- **Robust numerical discretizations**: spectral finite element methods (SEM), implicit-explicit time schemes, perfectly matched layers (PML), local time-stepping, high-order methods; development of the research code [OndoMathX](https://team.inria.fr/ananke/).
- **Inverse problems**: observer-based algorithms and optimal control approaches for wave-based inversion, applied to ultrasonic elastography (cardiac and corneal), MRI elastography, and hemodynamic reconstruction.
- **Applications**: cardiac imaging (ANR [ElastoHeart](https://anr.fr/), PI), corneal elastography, ultrasonic non-destructive testing, ocean wave and tsunami modelling.

## Short biography

I defended my PhD in January 2012 on the *Mathematical and numerical modelling of piezoelectric sensors*, carried out at [CEA-List](https://www.cea.fr/english/Pages/research-areas/information-technologies/digitalization-and-numerical-simulation.aspx) and supervised by [Patrick Joly](https://who.rocq.inria.fr/Patrick.Joly/) and Gary Cohen (Inria [POems](https://poems.math.cnrs.fr/) team). I then held a post-doctoral position at [Columbia University](https://www.columbia.edu/) (New York, 2012–2013) before joining Inria Saclay as a permanent researcher in September 2013. I obtained my *Habilitation à Diriger des Recherches* (HDR) in October 2022 on *Mathematical and numerical methods for double-scale wave propagation problems*.

I have co-supervised **13 PhD students** and collaborate closely with [CEA-List](https://www.cea.fr/) on industrial applications of wave simulation. I am the principal investigator of the **ANR ElastoHeart** project (~470k€), in collaboration with [PhysMed (Inserm)](https://www.inserm.fr/) and [IMRB (Inserm)](https://www.inserm.fr/), aiming to develop mathematical and numerical tools for cardiac elastography.

Since November 2024 I hold a part-time teaching position (*Professeur chargé de cours*, PCC, 64h) at the [CMAP, École polytechnique](https://portail.polytechnique.edu/cmap/en).
