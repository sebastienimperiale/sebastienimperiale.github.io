My research focuses on the mathematical and numerical analysis of PDEs, with emphasis on wave propagation in multi-scale media. A common thread is the co-existence of two or more scales — geometric (thin domains), physical (wave speed contrast), or parametric (homogenization). I address these problems through two complementary approaches: asymptotic analysis and robust numerical discretizations.

1. Multi-scale asymptotic analysis of wave propagation

Many physically relevant wave problems involve distinct scales. I study such double-scale problems by asymptotic analysis, characterising the limit equations and deriving error estimates. A unifying abstract framework for these problems is developed in my HDR and in [43] (MMS SIAM, 2025).

Application domains:

• Piezoelectricity: asymptotic models for thin bars and sensor/emitter devices [2, 6]

• Electromagnetism in cables: skin-effect asymptotics, multi-conductor cables [4, 8, 12, 27]

• Soft tissue mechanics: incompressible elastodynamics limit, nearly-incompressible hyperelastic solids [19, 28, 44]

• Gravito-acoustics: new derivation of ocean wave models from compressible Euler equations [35, 40]

• Cardiac electrophysiology: homogenisation of the bidomain model under mechanical deformation [17, 24]

• Abstract framework: unified treatment of stiff wave propagation problems [43]

Key collaborators: P. Joly, G. Beck (Inria POems); F. Caforio (Graz); J. Dubois, J. Sainte-Marie, A. Mangeney (Inria Ange); P. Moireau, D. Chapelle (Inria MEDISIM).

2. Robust numerical methods for wave equations

I design, analyse, and implement numerical schemes for time-dependent wave problems, with two main objectives: robust stability (CFL conditions independent of the small parameter) and high-order accuracy (typically order 4 in time). These methods are implemented in the OndoMathX research code.

Main contributions:

• Spectral element methods (SEM): mass-lumping, non-assembled computations, parametric meshes [14, 22]

• Implicit-explicit (IMEX) schemes: stability analysis for multi-scale hyperbolic systems [5, 7, 15, 25, 34, 46]

• Local time-stepping: conservative second-order local time discretization [31]

• Perfectly Matched Layers: stability for anisotropic acoustics and wave equations [1, 9, 23]

• High-order theta schemes and energy-preserving methods for dissipative systems [7, 15, 16, 25]

• SEM-FFT coupling: high-order spectral element FFT for the Poisson equation [22]

• Boundary stabilization: exponentially stable high-order discretizations [39]

Key collaborators: J. Chabassier, J. Diaz, G. Beck (Inria POems); J. Albella, J. Rodriguez (USC); F. Caforio; D. Baffet, M. Grote (Basel); A. Beni-Hamad; H. Methenni, A. Imperiale (CEA-List).

3. Inverse problems: elastography and hemodynamics

A growing part of my activity concerns wave-based inverse problems motivated by medical imaging and non-destructive testing. These topics are at the core of the ANR ElastoHeart project (PI, ~470k€, 2022–) and of the AnankΞ team.

Elastography: reconstruction of mechanical tissue properties from internal displacement measurements.

• Modelling of the Acoustic Radiation Force (ARF) in transient elastography [26]

• Observer-based algorithms for displacement reconstruction [21, 41, 42]

• Quasi-static MRI elastography: inverse problems for soft tissues (ongoing, with N. Chibli)

• Corneal optical coherence elastography [44, 45]

• Cardiac elastography (ANR ElastoHeart, with Z. Ramiche)

Hemodynamics: reconstruction of blood flow and pressure from distal measurements.

• Flow recovery via optimal control [36]

• Energy-consistent reduced-order cardiac models [32]

Non-destructive testing: estimation of pre-stress and loading conditions from guided wave measurements.

• Acoustoelasticity modelling [37]

• Kalman-based estimation [38]

• Data-assimilation of pre-stresses [37, 38]

Key collaborators: P. Moireau, J. Manganotti, T. Delaunay, Z. Ramiche, N. Chibli (Inria AnankΞ/MEDISIM); G. Merlini, J.-M. Allain (MEDISIM/CMAP); A. Dalmora, A. Imperiale (CEA-List); G. Bal, C. Bellis, F. Monard; M. Boulakia, M. de Buhan.

4. Spectral element methods: algorithms and software

I develop the research code OndoMathX, a high-order spectral finite element solver for wave propagation problems, designed around a non-assembled SEM strategy on hexahedral meshes. Key algorithmic contributions:

• SEM-FFT: coupling of high-order spectral elements with FFT for the Poisson equation [22]

• High-frequency stabilization: boundary terms preventing parasitic modes in observer discretizations [39]

• Parametric meshes: exact geometries for complex domains (cornea, cylinders)

The methods are also integrated in the CEA-List commercial software CIVA (via Alexandre Imperiale's parallel development), with two CEA-funded industrial studies on wave diffraction from rough surfaces.

Key collaborators: F. Caforio, J. Dubois, G. Merlini, Z. Ramiche (PhD student contributors); A. Imperiale (CEA-List).
